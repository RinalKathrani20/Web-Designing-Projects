About India

India is a land of ancient civilization, vibrant traditions, spiritual depth, and extraordinary geographical diversity.

OVERVIEW

Country Snapshot

India is a vast country in South Asia, known for unity in diversity. People of different languages, religions, and cultures live together while preserving shared national identity.
It is one of the world's largest and most populated democracies, balancing ancient traditions with modern growth in technology, infrastructure, and education.

HERITAGE

Histroy and Background

India has one of the oldest civilizations in the world, beginning with the Indus Valley Civilization. Over centuries, major dynasties and kingdoms advanced art, science, literature, administration, and architecture.
After colonial rule, India gained independence in 1947 and established a democratic republic. Today, the country protects its historical legacy while expanding as a modern nation.

IDENTITY

Culture and Tradition

India's culture reflects thousands of years of living traditions. Respect for elders, strong family values, hospitality, and community celebrations remain central to social life.

Music, dance, literature, handicrafts, and regional customs shape everyday identity, while people continue balancing cultural pride with modern lifestyles.

CELEBRATION

Festivals

Indian festivals celebrate faith, seasons, harvests, and cultural traditions. Major festivals include Diwali, Holi, Eid, Christmas, Navratri, Durga Puja, Pongal, Baisakhi, and Onam.

Festivals promote social harmony, preserve traditions, and strengthen the spirit of unity across regions and communities.

LANDSCAPE

Geography

India's geography includes the Himalayas, Indo-Gangetic plains, Thar Desert, Deccan Plateau, and an extensive coastline along the Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and Indian Ocean.

This diversity supports varied climates, ecosystems, agriculture, forests, and wildlife, making India one of the most geographically rich countries in the world.
